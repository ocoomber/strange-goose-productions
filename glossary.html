Formats
The shapes and qualities of video files. If a platform asks for something, it's in here.
9:16vertical
Vertical video. The shape of your phone held normally. Used by Reels, TikTok, Stories.
Every client gets a 9:16 cut from the same shoot, at no extra cost.
16:9widescreen
Widescreen. Standard for YouTube, broadcast, and anything that lives on a monitor.
YouTube pre-roll and hero videos are 16:9.
1:1square
A square frame. Instagram feed posts and some LinkedIn content still want this.
Square cuts sit alongside photos in a feed better than widescreen.
Aspect ratio
The shape of the frame — width to height. 16:9 is widescreen, 1:1 is square, 9:16 is vertical.
We cut one shoot into every ratio you need.
4K
A resolution — very sharp, roughly 4000 pixels across. Looks good on every screen, now and later.
We shoot 4K as standard. Future-proof.
Frame ratefps
How many still images per second make up the video. 24fps looks cinematic, 60fps looks real.
We'll pick the right one for what you're making.
Safe area
The part of the frame nothing important can live outside of — because captions, chrome, or a swipe-up button might cover it.
Stories reserve a chunk at the top and bottom; we compose for it.

Category · 02 / 04

Process
The stages of making a video, in the order they happen.
Brief
A short document describing what you want. If you don't have one we'll build it from one conversation.
We write the brief based on what you tell us on the first call.
Treatment
Our creative response — what the video will feel like, look like, and say. Usually 1–3 pages.
Delivered before anything shoots. You sign off first.
Storyboard
A series of rough drawings showing each shot in order. Confirms what we'll actually film.
Helps you see the video before we make it. No surprises later.
Picture lock
The edit is final. No more changes to the cut. After this we do sound and colour.
Usually week 3 of a 4-week project.
Grade / colour
Adjusting the colour of the finished edit — contrast, warmth, skin tones. What makes something feel cinematic.
Done after picture lock. Changes the mood without changing the cut.
Mix
Balancing the sound — dialogue, music, effects — so it works on every speaker, from phone to cinema.
Last step before delivery.

Category · 03 / 04

Files & delivery
What actually lands in your inbox at the end.
Cut-down
A shorter version of the main film — 30 seconds or 15 seconds cut from a 60-second master.
We edit each one as its own piece, not just a trim.
Deliverable
Any file we hand over at the end — the master, cut-downs, stills, subtitled versions.
Named by platform. Upload-ready. No renaming needed.
Pre-roll
An ad that plays before the thing someone came to watch — usually on YouTube.
Typically 15 or 30 seconds, often skippable after 5.
Master file
The full-quality original of the final video. Everything else is made from it.
Kept on our drives. Available to you on request, forever.
MP4
The standard video file format. Works on every platform. This is what you upload.
Every deliverable is an MP4 unless you need something else.

Category · 04 / 04

Money & rights
The practical bits about cost, use, and what you own.
Fixed quote
A single price for the whole job, agreed before we shoot. Doesn't change unless the brief does.
Agreed up front. No surprise invoices.
Usage rights
Where and how long you can use the finished video. We keep it simple: as long as you want, everywhere.
Some agencies charge more for TV vs. web. We don't.
Music licensing
Paying to use a specific piece of music legally. Needed for anything that isn't original.
We'll recommend cleared tracks, or write something custom.
50 / 50
Our payment terms: half up front on agreement, half on final delivery.
No retainer. No monthly fees. One project at a time.
